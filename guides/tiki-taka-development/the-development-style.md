# Tiki-Taka Development Style

![Tiki Taka the Development Style](/img/guides/tiki_taka_banner.png)

_A high-skill, low-friction, fast-iteration delivery model inspired by the football philosophy._

> Millenials will relate to this: Think **Barcelona (Pep era)**, **Spain 2008–2012**, **Man City’s positional play**: short passes, constant movement, minimal wasted energy — but deadly execution.
> Tiki-Taka Development is the same: **fluid collaboration, rapid delivery, and clean handoffs**, powered by **elite multi-talented people**.

---

## 1) What Tiki-Taka Means (in Software)

**Tiki-Taka = build high-quality products through nimble, low-overhead iterations**, where:

- Work moves like **quick passes**, not long slow “handoffs”
- Collaboration is **activated on-demand**, then released
- Each contributor is a **star player** with **strong fundamentals + versatility**
- The team **dances around the user**, adapting fast instead of committing to fragile assumptions

**Core idea:**

> _Keep momentum high. Keep ownership clear. Keep collaboration lightweight._

---

## 2) Why This Exists (The Problem It Solves)

Traditional delivery models often suffer from:

- Hard handoffs that require “training the next person”
- Teams stuck waiting for approvals, dependencies, or ceremonies
- Long commitments to assumptions that change by Week 2
- Overstaffing or underutilization

Tiki-Taka fixes this by running a **talent pool** like an elite midfield:

- Everyone can **receive the ball**, **control it**, and **progress play**
- You call in a teammate **only when needed**
- Then release them back to their own “star projects”

---

## 3) The Talent Pool Model (Star Players Only)

Tiki-Taka relies on a **small pool of mid-to-senior multitalented people** across:

- **Design**
- **Development**
- **Project management / delivery leadership**
- **QA mindset**
- **DevOps / deployment awareness**

### What “Multitalented” Means Here

Not “everyone does everything badly” — but:

- Each person owns their main specialty
- And can **operate across adjacent roles** without slowing down the system

**Example:**
A designer who can also shape UX flows + define acceptance criteria.
A dev who can also spec features + manage release risk.
A PM who can also validate scope + unblock technical decisions.

---

## 4) On-Need Collaboration (“The Pass”)

Instead of permanently assigning people to a project:

### Tiki-Taka Collaboration Rule

**Activate talent only for the required duration and effort.**

That means:

- Pull in a specialist for a _specific outcome_
- Get the value
- Release them immediately

This reduces:

- Idle time
- Meetings
- “Ownership confusion”
- Coordination overhead

**Football translation:**
You don’t keep 6 players crowding the ball.
You pass, move, create space, finish.

---

## 5) Relay Handoffs (Not Hard Handoffs)

Tiki-Taka avoids heavy transitions like:

> “Here’s a 40-page spec, now train the next person.”

Instead it uses **relay handoffs**, where the work is transferred with minimal friction:

### Relay Handoff Format (Fast + Clear)

- **Current state** (what exists)
- **Next pass** (what to do next)
- **Definition of done**
- **Risks / gotchas**
- **Links** (PRs, Figma, tickets)

No onboarding ceremony. No long training sessions.
Just **clean control and forward motion**.

---

## 6) Async-First by Default (Play Without Everyone Online)

Tiki-Taka assumes talent is distributed and busy.

So it’s built for:

- Deep work
- Minimal meetings
- Fast updates
- Clear written decisions

### Async Standards

- Decisions are written and timestamped
- Work is visible (board, issues, PRs)
- Updates are lightweight but consistent
- Reviews happen in short cycles

**If your team can’t work async, you can’t play tiki-taka.**

---

## 7) Minimal Commitment to Assumptions (Stay Agile, Not Fragile)

Tiki-Taka borrows from Agile, but it’s more ruthless about **not marrying assumptions**.

### Key behavior

- Prototype quickly
- Validate with users early
- Keep scope adjustable
- Iterate like you’re **looking for the through-ball**, not forcing a long shot

**We don’t defend assumptions. We test them.**

---

## 8) How Work Moves (The Tiki-Taka Flow)

### The Loop

1. **Receive**: understand the user need (1–2 bullets, not essays)
2. **Control**: shape the smallest useful increment (MVP slice)
3. **Pass**: bring in the right talent briefly (design/dev/PM)
4. **Move**: implement + keep progress continuous
5. **Finish**: ship a working increment
6. **Reset**: learn → adjust → repeat

### Deliverables are always “playable”

Meaning: **usable, testable, demo-able**.

---

## 9) Sprint Structure (Lightweight, Not Ceremony-Heavy)

Tiki-Taka can run inside sprints, but doesn’t worship them.

### Recommended cadence

- **1–2 week sprint**
- Daily async check-in
- One short weekly sync if needed

### Minimum ceremonies

- **Kickoff** (15–30 min): goal + constraints + first pass plan
- **Demo** (15–30 min): show working product
- **Retro** (15–20 min): one improvement only

No meeting inflation.

---

## 10) What Makes Tiki-Taka Different (The Benchmarks)

### Compared to “Classic Agile/Scrum”

- Less ceremony, more execution
- Less role rigidity, more versatility
- Less dependency waiting, more on-need activation

### Compared to “Kanban”

- Still flow-based, but more intentional about **short tactical collaborations**
- Stronger emphasis on **elite skill + speed of control**

### Compared to “Waterfall”

- Assumption-light
- Change-friendly
- Built for learning while building

---

## 11) Quality Rules (Because Flair Without Discipline is Chaos)

Tiki-Taka is fun — but it’s not sloppy.

### Non-negotiables

- Small PRs, reviewed fast
- Clean naming + readable code
- Test critical flows (even minimal)
- Ship in increments, not mega-releases
- Observability mindset (logs, errors, performance)

**Tiki-taka is precision, not randomness.**

---

## 12) Who This Style Works Best For

Tiki-Taka thrives when the team is:

✅ Mid-to-senior
✅ Highly skilled (strong intuition + fundamentals)
✅ Multi-talented (adjacent skills)
✅ Async-capable
✅ Comfortable with ambiguity
✅ Fast at decision-making

It struggles when the team requires:

❌ Heavy supervision
❌ Long trainings for handoffs
❌ Constant meetings to progress
❌ Strict role boundaries

---

## 13) The “Player Types” in the Pool (Fun but Useful)

- **The Pivot (Busquets role):** stabilizes architecture, removes chaos
- **The Playmaker (Xavi role):** orchestrates flow, makes clean decisions
- **The Progressor (Iniesta role):** breaks complexity into shippable slices
- **The Finisher (Messi role):** turns effort into shipped outcomes fast
- **The Fullback Engine (Alves role):** boosts delivery with tooling + automation
- **The Keeper (Casillas role):** protects reliability, releases safely

Everyone can pass.
A few can finish instantly.

---

## 14) Tiki-Taka Rules of Play (Simple + Memorable)

1. **Short passes > long throws**
2. **Move the work forward daily**
3. **Activate talent only when needed**
4. **Relay handoffs, not training handoffs**
5. **Async by default**
6. **Validate assumptions early**
7. **Ship working increments**
8. **Quality is part of speed**
